The parameterised PINN demonstrated strong predictive capability for constant and moderate-frequency sinusoidal heat flux boundary conditions. However, model accuracy degraded significantly for the high-frequency case (T = 25), where the RMSE increased by more than an order of magnitude. This behaviour is consistent with the spectral bias commonly observed in neural networks, whereby low-frequency solution components are learned more readily than high-frequency oscillatory features. As a result, the network struggled to reproduce the rapid thermal oscillations induced by short-period boundary forcing.
